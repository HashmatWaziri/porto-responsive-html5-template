You are an expert web developer, UX designer, content strategist, and brand architect specializing in industrial manufacturing websites. You are tasked with creating a comprehensive, world-class website for **Fanavar Steel** (فن‌آور), Afghanistan's first modern, full-scale metal fabrication factory. This prompt provides complete context including business philosophy, institutional knowledge, competitive insights, and strategic positioning to guide the entire website creation process—from initial design concepts through final implementation.

**Critical Understanding**: This is not a generic industrial website. ### 1.2 Company Story and Origins

**The Name**: "Fanavar" (فن‌آور) translates to "Innovator" in Dari/Farsi. The tagline "Novin" (نوین) reinforces commitment to innovation and modernity. This name was chosen as a mission statement—bringing new capabilities, new standards, and new possibilities to Afghan manufacturing.

**The Founding Moment**: During a 2019 trip to the United Arab Emirates, while exploring opportunities to purchase modern machinery, the founders identified a critical gap in Afghanistan's cutting and laser services market. This realization came after years of customers approaching them with requests they could not fulfill due to lack of specialized equipment.

**The Founders**: Three business partners—Nazir, Shafiq, and Tahir Hamidi—each bringing complementary strengths rooted in diverse international experience across China, Russia, and Afghanistan. This cross-cultural exposure equipped them with unique perspective on industrial development.

**The Evolution**: While Fanavar Steel was officially established in 2024, the team's professional roots trace back over a decade through Soroush Company, which operated primarily as a trading company before the need for a dedicated production facility became apparent.

## Part 3: Lessons Learned from Comprehensive Competitor Analysis

### 3.1 Critical Success Factors (Industry Standards)

**Visual Design Requirements** (100% of 21 competitors implement):

1. **Color Schemes and Brand Identity**:
   - Consistent, professional color schemes communicating reliability and expertise
   - Most effective: Blue/white (trust, professionalism), Dark gray/black with orange/yellow accents (visual hierarchy), Dark teal with orange (stability + energy)
   - **MANDATORY**: Consistent color application across ALL pages

2. **Photography Standards**:
   - High-quality images of manufacturing facilities, machinery, production processes
   - Professional photography of completed projects and installations
   - Worker-focused imagery demonstrating human expertise
   - Equipment close-ups showcasing technological capability
   - **CRITICAL**: Every successful competitor invests significantly in professional photography

3. **Typography Standards**:
   - Large, clear headings (24-48px) for page titles and section headers
   - Consistent font families throughout (professional sans-serif recommended)
   - Adequate line height and paragraph spacing for readability
   - Bold emphasis on key value propositions and statistics
   - Clear distinction between headings and body text
   
   **Multilingual Typography Requirements** (MANDATORY):
   - **Dari**: Persian/Nastaliq script fonts (Vazirmatn, Behdad, Tahoma) with +10% font size adjustment
   - **Pashto**: Pashto script fonts (PDMS_Saleem, Nastaliq) with +15% font size adjustment
   - **English**: Standard professional fonts (Tahoma, Arial, system fonts)
   - Proper rendering of all character sets and diacritics
   - Increased line height for RTL scripts for optimal readability
   - RTL text alignment and direction support

**Navigation and Information Architecture** (Industry Standards):

1. **Primary Navigation Structure** (6-8 primary navigation items + Language Switcher):
   - Home (100% of competitors)
   - About Us/Company (95%)
   - Products/Services (100%)
   - Projects/Portfolio (80%)
   - Contact Us (100%)
   - Capabilities/Technology (60%)
   - Industries/Sectors (55%)
   - Resources/Downloads/Catalogs (50%)
   - **Language Switcher** (Dari, Pashto, English) - MUST be prominently placed in header navigation

2. **Footer Standards** (100% of competitors):
   - Multiple-column layouts (3-4 columns)
   - Quick links in organized categories
   - Complete contact information (phone, email, address)
   - Social media links with consistent iconography
   - Embedded Google Maps (80% of competitors)

**Content Organization Standards**:

1. **Hero Sections** (100% of competitors):
   - Full-width background images or videos
   - Large, clear page titles (40-60% of viewport height)
   - Value proposition messaging or taglines
   - Prominent call-to-action buttons

2. **Service/Product Presentation**:

2. **Certifications** (86% of competitors):
   - ISO certifications (ISO 9001:2015 most common)
   - Industry-specific certifications

3. **Social Proof** (76% of competitors):
   - Named testimonials with client titles and companies
   - Client logo displays showing market reach
   - Case studies with detailed project information

### 3.2 Universal Weaknesses (Critical Differentiation Opportunities)

**PRIMARY WEAKNESS: Mobile Optimization** (0% of competitors demonstrate visible mobile-optimized layouts)

**Critical Finding**: 100% of competitors show desktop-focused designs that may not adapt properly to mobile devices.

**Required Mobile-First Implementations**:
- **Mobile hamburger menu** with full-screen overlay, solid background, clear visibility, and inline contact form
- **Burger menu positioning**: LEFT side for English (LTR), RIGHT side for Dari/Pashto (RTL)
- **Language switcher** easily accessible and functional within mobile menu
- Touch targets meeting 44x44 pixel minimum
- Sticky header for easy navigation access
- Single-column layouts that stack appropriately
- Responsive grid systems that adapt to screen width
- Images that scale proportionally without breaking layout
- Typography that scales for readability on all devices
- Fast-loading mobile experience

**SECONDARY WEAKNESS: Support Hours Visibility** (0% of competitors prominently display support hours on all pages)

**Critical Finding**: 95% of competitors provide no visible support hours information at all. This represents a major trust-building and differentiation opportunity.

**Required Implementation**:
- Support hours prominently visible on ALL pages (not just footer or contact page)
- Clear, consistent formatting across all pages
- Integration with contact information
- Multiple time zone support if applicable

**Additional Weaknesses to Address**:
- Information density: Avoid 8-10 major sections causing cognitive overload
- Content repetition: Ensure unique content on each page
- Contact information placement: Make contact information accessible without requiring navigation to footer

### 3.3 Contact Accessibility Standards

**CRITICAL: Top Contact Bar - The First Thing Users See** (MANDATORY IMPLEMENTATION):

The very first element visible on every page of the website must be a horizontal contact information bar positioned at the absolute top of the page, above all other content including the logo, navigation menu, and hero section. This bar must be immediately noticeable and easily accessible.

**Visual Requirements**:
- **Position**: Fixed at the very top of the viewport, above all other page elements
- **Background Color**: Dark gray or black background (Steel Gray Dark #2D3748 or Charcoal #1A202C) to create strong visual contrast and ensure visibility
- **Height**: Sufficient height (minimum 40-50px) to be clearly visible and clickable on all devices
- **Full Width**: Spans the entire width of the browser window
- **Typography**: Clear, readable text with adequate contrast (white or light text on dark background)
- **Iconography**: Social media icons must be clearly visible and properly sized for easy clicking

**Content Layout** (Left and Right Distribution):
- **Left Side**: 
  - Phone number with "Call us:" label (e.g., "Call us: +93 XX XXX XXXX")
  - Email address with "Email:" label (e.g., "Email: info@fanavarsteel.com")
  - Both should be clearly separated and easy to read
- **Right Side**:
  - "Follow us:" text label
  - Social media icons (Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube, Instagram - as applicable)
  - Icons should be properly spaced and sized for easy clicking/tapping

**Visibility and Accessibility**:
- Must be visible on page load without scrolling
- Must remain visible or accessible on all pages (sticky/fixed positioning recommended)
- Must be responsive and functional on mobile devices (icons and text must be touch-friendly)
- Must maintain visibility across all screen sizes (desktop, tablet, mobile)
- Text and icons must meet WCAG contrast requirements for accessibility

**Why This Matters**:
This top contact bar addresses the fundamental user need for immediate access to contact information. When potential customers visit the website, they should be able to see and access phone number, email, and social media links instantly, without having to search through navigation menus or scroll to the footer. This creates trust, demonstrates professionalism, and removes barriers to communication.

**Multi-Channel Contact Requirements** (Industry Standards):
- **Top Contact Bar** (as specified above) - MANDATORY first element on all pages
- Prominent phone number in header and footer (100% of competitors)
- Email address with clear labeling and departmental differentiation (info@, sales@, support@)
- Contact form on Contact Us page (100%)
- WhatsApp integration with floating button (52% of competitors)
- Physical address with map integration (86% of competitors)
- Social media links in header and footer (95% of competitors)

- Letter spacing: Appropriate for Persian script characteristics

- Proper rendering of Pashto-specific characters
- Line height: Increased for script readability
- Letter spacing: Appropriate for Pashto script characteristics

- Standard sizing and spacing
